01 — Logo Suite

Two lockups, one light.

Everything is built around one mark: a glowing gold sun. It appears full-size as the hero and compact as the nav lockup. Both breathe. Nothing else is a logo.

I’m Riley.

Marketing, splash, app launch, social. Sun above the wordmark. Files: meet-riley.png (static) · meet-riley-breathing.gif/.mp4/.html (animated).

Secondary · Nav
Riley.

Nav bars, app headers, sidebars, favicons. Sun to the left of the word. Files: riley-nav-logo.png (transparent) · riley-nav-logo.html (animated).

The sun

A single gold orb — sunrise at 8:14, the moment light returns. It is the whole identity distilled: warmth, hope, a new day. It gently breathes so the brand always feels alive, never static.

02 — Clear Space & Minimum Size

Give it room to glow.

The glow is part of the logo — never crop it. Keep clear space of at least one sun-diameter on every side. The light must always fall on a dark or deep background.

05 — Typography

Serif for soul, sans for clarity.

Three faces, each with a job. Never substitute — all three are free on Google Fonts.

06 — Motion

It breathes.

The sun has one signature motion — a slow, calm breath. It signals a presence that’s alive and unhurried. Use it wherever the mark can animate; never speed it up or make it bounce.

Breathe

@keyframes breathe{
  0%, 100% { transform: scale(1);    opacity: .92 }
  50%      { transform: scale(1.06); opacity: 1   }
}
/* 5s · ease-in-out · infinite */

Duration 5s, ease-in-out, infinite. Scale 1 → 1.06, opacity 0.92 → 1. Slow enough to feel like a real breath.

07 — Voice & Tone

How Riley speaks.

The words carry the brand as much as the light does. Riley is a steady friend who has been there — present, honest, never clinical.

We are

  • Whole-person — grief, burnout, overwhelm, recovery. Recovery is one chapter, never the whole story.
  • Warm, honest, unhurried. A friend, not a clinician.
  • Trauma-aware and inclusive — we never assume anyone’s gender, pronouns, faith, or that substances are their only struggle.
  • Hopeful without pretending everything is fine.

We are not

  • Toxic positivity, hustle, or "good vibes only."
  • Preachy, clinical, or diagnostic. No medical claims.
  • Shaming, judgmental, or fear-based.
  • Sobriety-only. That framing erases most of who we serve.
In one line
"You’re not starting over. You’re continuing — and you don’t have to do it alone."
