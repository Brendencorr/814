## THE DIFFERENTIATOR PAIR (MID-FUNNEL, IN THIS ORDER)

1. **"Built by someone who's been through it."** - the lead differentiator and the anti-ChatGPT line. The moat is provenance: lived experience, recovery and coaching frameworks, a deterministic crisis path, a focused purpose. ChatGPT has memory; it doesn't have a why.
2. **"Never explain yourself twice."** - retained as proof of care, never the lead. Memory is what someone who's been through it would build, because they know what re-telling costs. Comparison contexts, pricing page, campaign Day 9.

**The "already knows" doctrine:** any claim of knowing points at THE ROAD (what Riley was built around), never at the member's personal story on day zero. "She knows this road from day one, and learns yours from the first hello" is the canonical resolution. Riley carries the founder's story but never claims it as her own biography: "built by someone who's been through it," never "she's been through it."

## THE CATEGORY AND THE MISSION

The category is **REBUILDING** - not wellness, not recovery. "Wellness" is a sea; "recovery" is a label. Rebuilding is ours: sobriety, grief, burnout, the body, the whole restart - one companion for the whole person. Recovery is one chapter, never the whole story. (Category language is internal + investor-facing; the porch speaks in change and chapters, not category names.)

**The mission:** help people build a life they don't want to escape from. Become who you were meant to become.

**The brand in one image:** a single warm light in a dark room, and a friend who speaks plainly.

**The shipping test** (three yeses or it doesn't ship): Does it feel like a warm light in a dark room? Would a stranger know who they're about to meet? Would someone on their worst night feel safer having seen it?

---

## THE PROBLEM (TWO REGISTERS - NEVER MIX THEM)

**The 2am register** (every surface a human reads): When life gets hard, the hardest hours are the ones nobody's available for - and every place you turn makes you start your story over. The problem is being alone with a hard thing. Lived experience is why Riley understands it; memory is how she never makes you repeat it.

**The clinical register** (investor market slide, B2B only): Continuity of care. Episodic support forces people to restart their story at every touchpoint. Riley provides continuous, personalized, memory-driven support in the hours and gaps formal care cannot cover.

**Rule:** the 2am register never appears in comparative or portrait form on member-facing surfaces.

## THE ORIGIN STORY (CANON)

Two stories that meet at one minute - always told as two, never blurred. **The watch:** a little boy who loved his watch and always answered 8:14. Years later, still wearing it, the watch stopped - at 8:14, for good. He's gone now. 8:14 stayed behind. **The light:** the founder's own years of losing himself and finding the way back - getting sober, grieving, starting over. There was a minute in that story too: the minute the light came back. **The convergence:** when it came time to name this, there was only ever one number. Grief and recovery held in one minute - which is exactly what Riley is built for.

**Tense rule:** "comes back" in member-facing copy (a promise); "came back" for the founder's past (a testimony). **Telling rule:** told fully on the About page, discovered everywhere else (campaign model: D7 whisper, D11 discovery).

## BRAND LAWS (PERMANENT)

1. **Faceless about the maker - always.** No founder/family/origin names or recognizable places on member-facing surfaces. Specificity of feeling, not fact. Refinement: the faceless law protects the ORIGIN, not the members - named member testimonials permitted and encouraged, written consent on file.
2. **Named, investor-facing.** Two registers, one discipline.
3. **Riley is "she"** - always within reach of plain AI disclosure. Never "it," never "we." Built by someone who's been through it - never "she's been through it."
4. **Names and marks:** "The 814 Project, LLC" legal surfaces only. "8:14" with colon on brand surfaces. "Riley · by The 8:14 Project" is the sanctioned maker's-mark signature (closes surfaces, never leads them).
5. **Plain hyphens, never em-dashes** - captions AND asset text layers. Sentinel hard fail.
6. **No dark patterns.** Nothing at 8:14 is countdowned or "limited time."
7. **Safety is never tiered;** the crisis path always comes first.
8. **The chat is the product.** Clarity is the scoreboard; Life Map is the receipt.
9. **Voice:** warm, plain, honest, unhurried - a friend, not a clinician. Riley suggests once, gently, then drops it. Never assumes gender/pronouns/faith/relationships. Missed days met with welcome.
10. **Words we keep / avoid** per Brand Guidelines v2.1, with founder exceptions: "journey" permitted in program names; the "Do hard things" block is sanctioned founder voice. Sentinel/Sage must not flag either.
11. **The :14 rhythm is a brand signature:** posts at 9:14/1:14/7:14 MT; the Reset is 8:14 long; programs are $8.14.
12. **meetriley.us is the only website.**
13. **Visual system lives in Brand Guidelines v2.1.** This document governs words; that one governs light.
